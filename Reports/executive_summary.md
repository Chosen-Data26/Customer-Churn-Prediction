# From the Heatmap
Insight 1: Customers with lower platform engagement, measured by login frequency, are significantly more likely to churn.
Insight 2: Customer engagement metrics form a strong correlated cluster, suggesting that highly engaged users consistently interact across multiple touchpoints.
Insight 3: High cart abandonment behavior is associated with lower engagement and is a potential early indicator of churn risk.
Insight 4: Customer lifetime value is strongly driven by engagement and purchase behavior, indicating that increasing user interaction directly impacts revenue.
Insight 5: Churn is primarily influenced by behavioral patterns rather than demographic characteristics, highlighting the importance of user activity monitoring.
